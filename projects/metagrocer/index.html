App Overview

Plan. Shop. Save.

We understand that shopping can be a hassle some times, and shopping for good deals is even harder. That is why we designed Metagrocer - to help you plan your shopping trip, get coupons for your items, and use them at the store. Whether you are making a quick run to the store for a few items, planning your weekly grocery shopping, or spontaneously deciding to buy yourself a snack as you walk by the store, Metagrocer will help you find the best deals.

Design Process

How we created the design - from beginning to end.

  • Interaction Design

  • 1. Empathize

    Contextual Inquiry & Interview

    Observed and interviewed 3 UW students while they went on their grocery shopping trips.

  • 2. Define

    Tasks

    Developed six tasks that could be performed with our app.

  • 3. Ideate

    Design Sketches

    Sketched three interface designs that supported the tasks, and selected one to be further refined.

  • 4. Prototype

    Storyboard

    Created two storyboards to convey the functionality of the design.

  • Interface Design

  • 5. Prototype

    Paper prototype

    Created paper prototype that conveyed the critical aspects of our design.

  • 6. Evaluation

    Nielsen's Heuristic Evaluation

    Received two heuristic evaluations and revised the paper prototype based on the evaluation results.

  • 7. Test

    Usability Testing

    Conducted three usability testing sessions with the paper prototype.

  • 8. Improve

    Digital Mockup

    Updated prototype with the feedback and created digital mockup.

Price Comparison

Using our app, you can easily compare the price of a single item between multiple stores. All it requires is searching and viewing the item details page. You can also compare how much it will cost to purchase all the items in your shopping list if you shop at just one store, or at multiple stores. All you have to do is make the shopping list, and the app will tell you which store to go to in order to get all the items on your list for the best price.

Gamification

After the end of each shopping trip, you will have the opportunity to scan your receipt in order to earn points and level up. The more receipts you scan, the higher level you achieve. These scanned receipts are then used to show other users how much items cost, and what kinds of deals they can get. You will even be able to see the number of people that your receipt scanning has helped!

Coupons to go

If you are in a hurry but still want to save money, you can scan the barcode of the items you are purchasing, and the coupons for those items will automatically be added to your phone. All you need to do then is scan the coupons at checkout! If you are shopping with a list instead, you don't even have to scan the item to get the coupon added.
